Problem

What is the problem?

Although artificial intelligence has proved to be of much utility in our daily lives, it poses many concerns in its intersection with academia. Specifically, by endowing commonplace students with the ability to instantaneously generate educational work, AI has led to the widespread compromise of authenticity, critical thought, and informational falsity as a whole.

In accordance with this idea, the "Office of Academic Integrity" of Harvard University, a esteemed institution holding reputability in the areas of research and instruction, clearly delineates the challenges rendered by AI in academic settings, as featured in the quote shown to the right.

In the context of this quote, it is elaborated on how the existence of this tool may:

  • Incentivize students to present inauthentic, artificially-generated work
  • Make the detection of genuine thought exponentially more difficult
  • Inhibit constructive deliberation and intellectual development, obtaining the benefits of which typically serves as the objective for such assignments
“On the other hand, the integration of AI in academic settings raises pressing concerns about academic integrity, as these powerful tools can sometimes undermine the authenticity of student work and blur the lines of original thought.”

(“Academic Integrity” 1)
