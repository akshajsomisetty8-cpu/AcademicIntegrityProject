What are the root causes?

Academic Competence/Heightened Educational Rigor

Pedagogical factors (such as the assignment of lengthy essays, generalized written prompts, or rote tasks, all of which may be easily addressed via artificial intelligence) play a key role in the stimulation of excessive and academically unethical AI usage.

Combined with the obscurity of the boundaries separating academic assistance from academic dishonesty as well as individually eccentric stressors, the pressure exerted by such tasks has been found to facilitate the gradual erosion of integrity via AI.

Research - PubMed

This is as demonstrated in a study featured in PubMed, an encyclopedic database comprised by millions of abstracts managed by the National Institute of Health.

“In total, 5 major themes were identified as drivers of AI dependency: institutional workload (reported by >80% [37/46] of participants), low academic confidence (noted by 28/46, 60%), and perfectionism-related stress (23/46, 50%)”

Magnified Accessibility to Artificial Intelligence

On the whole, such problems have also arisen due to the commonization and wide accessibility of AI. This is because, having a resource of rapidity, accuracy, and eloquence directly within reach, students tend to gravitate toward that which can optimize their time and grade alike, which, in the contemporary academic era of complex questioning and thought-evocative work, can only be done by AI.

Research - ScienceDirect

As per the content of a website featured by ScienceDirect, a database constituted of over 18 million scientific publications and widely noted for its consolidation of validity through extensive peer-review, it is stated how students utilize artificial intelligence, in large part, as a panacea for their academic struggles as well as the health-related disadvantages that said difficulties engender.
