Solution

What are the identified solutions?

Although it may seem relatively impossible to address such a problem, it may be of use to know that the most expansive dilemmas can be resolved with the implementation of the smallest idea; as such, by emphasizing and reforming the tolerance of AI in the classroom itself, society as a whole can prevent the aggregation of incidents reforming academic misconduct into a global concern.

Expanding off of this concept, potentially productive solutions include:

  • Transition toward the implementation of live presentations, in-class essays, and other assignments
  • Necessitate the evident production of authentic, ethical work (intuitive in its response to this problem, for while completing an assignment in a supervised, controlled environment, students are made to rely on their own intelligence to expedite academic progression)
  • Encourage and commonize interactive, communicational assessments (such as seminars or class discussions) evoking genuine thought and meaningful ideas whilst evincing the exhibition of colloquial skills

Rationale

These approaches will be of utility in serving as solutions due to their facile applicability in the classroom as well as their encouragement(and necessitation) of genuine learning.

Although their efficiency may be debated in some circumstances, they are aptly reinforced by their practicality, for their utilization only requires a shift in the type of work assigned and the quantity of pertinent academic information disclosed.

Considering the continual proliferation of artificial resources as well as the increasing pervasion of its influence in all sectors of society, such responses may be of the optimum in further maintaining academic integrity.

Magnitudinous Impact

In line with an article (from the renowned American Psychological Association, which is widely known as the largest organization of psychologists in the world) authored by a specialist holding a PhD in experimental psychology, the sweeping lack of cognizance about integrity policies has also played a pivotal role in the materialization of academic falsification.

“In interviews with students who were reported for cheating, Beasley (2014) found that many students were not familiar with the policies or the consequences of academic misconduct and believed that if the instructor had discussed the policy and reminded them, it would have deterred them from cheating.”

The regulation of cheating through precautionary measures is almost tantamount to the introduction of authentic assessments; as delineated in this website, which has referenced a multitude of relative studies in the reinforcement of its speculation, a marked review of these policies is stated to be integral in the dissuasion of students from engaging in academic misconduct, for, with an informed perspective, the decisions made by one will have been deliberated with elements of greater clarity and logic.
